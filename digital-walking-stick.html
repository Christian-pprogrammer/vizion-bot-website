KEY FEATURES OF THE DIGITAL WALKING STICK

Vizion Bot develops a digital walking stick for visually impaired people, featuring auto-sensing, vibration alerts, adjustable tubes, and a solar-powered battery. Their goal is to improve accessibility, safety, and quality of life for people with visual impairments. Key features include:

⁠Obstacle Detection: Uses ultrasonic sensors to detect nearby obstacles and alert the user through vibration signals.

⁠⁠Vibration Alerts : Different vibration patterns indicate the distance to obstacles, helping users adjust their path.

⁠Lightweight & Foldable Design : Easy to carry, store, and unfold when needed.

Ergonomic Grip: Comfortable, non-slip handle for long periods of use.

⁠Rechargeable Battery: Long-lasting battery life, easily recharged via USB.

⁠Durable & Weather-Resistant: Designed for daily use in different weather conditions.

Adjustable Length: Telescopic shaft to suit users of different heights.

⁠Built-in LED Light: Illuminates the path in low-light environments.

Audible Alerts: Optional sound notification in addition to vibrations for obstacle warning.

GUIDELINES FOR USING THE DIGITAL WALKING STICK

Visually impaired individuals should follow these rules when using the digital walking stick:

⁠Wear the elastic strap located at the top of the stick.

⁠Turn on the stick by pressing the button on the left-side switch.

⁠Hold the stick in front of your foot while walking.

The stick will start to vibrate to indicate obstacles:

Low vibration: Detected at approximately 1 meter from obstacles.

High vibration with sound: Detected at 80–0 cm from obstacles.

Fold the stick when entering cars or other transport to protect it.

Turn off the stick when you reach your destination to save power.
